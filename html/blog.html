Reelin'

Written by Mr. M on Jan 12, 2015.

Hmmm decisions, decisions.Not with the brands per se, as there are only two i would part any cash for.No, the decision is fixed spool or baitcaster. It would be usual for me to favour the fixed spool,from my coarse fishing days, but the idea that jigging from the rocks might be involved would favour the quicker release freespooling from a baitcaster.But that Aernos is nice and could be at the right price, time will tell.

The decision has been made to get a baitcaster.From what i can understand from a distance, a lot of the shore fishing might be deep water jigging, from micro jigs to some mid-range ones (45-60g).Baitcaster reel orientation allows an much easier drop of the lure than casting line off a fixed spool, so i've opted for a Shimano Caenan 101.

The Caenan 101 is the left handed model of this baitcaster reel, and promises much smoothness for such a budget entry shimano reel, plus salt approved (whatever that proves to be).Only concern is the relative low amounts of drag.

For now i've got a cheap practise baitcaster i can trial/abuse at my local shore/pike venue before the final assault in May.

Hi-Viz

Written by Mr. M on Jan 12, 2015.

The first line up for trial is some Berkeley Whiplash in a daring hi-viz orange. Usually im all about stealth and prefer the clear lines wherever possible, this time i can see the need to see the line as the fish dart in and out of the rocky outcrops.I'll still be using fluorocarbon for the leader to retain presentation of the lure.

First impressions of the Whiplash line aren't all that favourable if finesse or supple was on the shopping list of requirements.Coarse texture and a very bumpy uneven feel as you rub your thumb/finger down the line.How this translates into actual use in the field is yet to be seen.Eh, let's not knock it down until its a tombola :)
